Difference between tests

Binomial

A binomial A/B test involves outcomes that can be categorized into two distinct groups, such as "success" or "failure," making it suitable for conversion rate tests where the outcome is binary.

Non-Binomial

Non-binomial A/B testing is used when the outcomes cannot be neatly divided into two categories and might involve multiple variables or continuous data, such as the amount of time spent on a page or the total revenue generated.

This type of testing is more complex and requires different statistical methods to analyze the results, as it deals with a range of outcomes rather than a simple yes/no scenario.
